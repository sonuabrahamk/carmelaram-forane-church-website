Carmel Mercy Home - History

Carmel Mercy Home for the aged is the charitable undertaking of the families of the Mount Carmel Forane Church, Carmelaram. This project is the faithful response of the parishioners to the words of Jesus, “I desire mercy, not sacrifice” (Mt 9:13). So its mission is to look after the elderly people with utmost care and empathy in a pleasant atmosphere to lead a peaceful life till their last journey. The foundation stone was laid on 8th December 2016 by Fr. Thomas Vadakkedath OCD, the first vicar of the Mt. Carmel Church, who wanted to provide services to the elderly for an active, dignified, and healthier life. Carmel Mercy Home (CMH) was blessed and inaugurated by Mar Sebastian Adayanthrath, the Bishop of Mandya on 2nd October 2021. Attached to this institute, a Divine Mercy Chapel was built, which was blessed by the then Bishop of Mandya Mar Anthony Kariyil CMI on 4th August 2019. The Mount Carmel Parish entrusted the pastoral and nursing care in the CMH to the Snehagiri Missionary Sisters (SMS). Accordingly, the superior of the SMS is one of the Trustees and director of CMH. CMH administration will be coordinated by governing body, administrative body (manager, director, assistant director secretary, & treasurer). Carmel Mercy Home Trust is responsible for the safety and security of the SMS at Carmel Mercy Home.
